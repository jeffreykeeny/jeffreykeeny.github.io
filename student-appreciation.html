STUDENT STAFF BANQUET

As the Graphic and Production Designer for the Associated Students of University of California (ASUC) Student Union, I received the amazing opportunity to spearhead the Student Staff Appreciation Banquet on December 9, 2015. I was given the responsibility to develop the overall theme, design materials, and marketing campaign for this event.

 

The ASUC Student Union advocates for UC Berkeley's students’ best interests by providing an inclusive environment for student development and learning that builds on UC Berkeley’s academic experience. The mission for the Student Union is twofold: first, they operate outside of the academics of UC Berkeley, choosing instead to focus on the Cal student experience; second, they challenge themselves to reach further and be innovative in the services and programs they offer students. The ASUC Student Union, in partnership with the ASUC and Graduate Assembly, supports student learning, development, and community building by collaborating and engaging with students, campus partners, and the greater community. As a sustainable and inclusive organization, the ASUC Student Union enhances the co-curricular experience at UC Berkeley. Lastly, the staff of the ASUC Student Union is primarily comprised of UC Berkeley students, including myself.

The Concept

Working with the lead event coordinator, Gabby McGrath, I brainstormed various ideas for the theme of the banquet. As it was approaching the winter holiday season, I thought it would only be fitting to have the theme revolve around a familiar sense of the holiday season.

 

One important and challenging obstacle that I faced when coming up with the overall theme was the audience the banquet was intended for. It was important to consider the fact that the student staff is comprised of individuals from very different cultures and backgrounds, and the theme had to fulfill this circumstance.

 

After close evaluation, I eventually settled on a sophisticated concept, one that embodied the experience of being indoors during the winter season — I called it “Pine.” My goal was to incorporate different, familiar holiday elements, while ensuring a neutral theme for the diverse demographic of the guests.

Photos courtesy of Pinterest

I was asked to lead the marketing campaign to publicize the banquet to all professional and student staff members. My team and I approached the marketing through digital and print mediums, which consequently required me to create digital signage and printed invitations.

 

Staying parallel to the theme of the event, I wanted to evoke a clean and sophisticated appearance with the design material. The color palette was neutral with golden, red, and winter green accents to underscore the winter atmosphere. I also spent a significant amount of time working with different intricate monoline ornaments to decorate all the design material that were published for this event.

 

Furthermore, the banquet was set up with multiple stations for the attendees to enjoy a variety of activities. The stations included a self-serve buffet and a thank you card writing table, each with its own design centerpiece that I was asked to create.

 

It was important to keep cohesion  between the Pine theme of the banquet and all the design material. Thus, I downloaded different images I found on-line and meticulously cut each element out from the photograph to incorporate into the designs. All of the elements, particularly leaves and flowers, present a sense of nature and the winter holiday.

 

The design on below was used as the centerpiece for the thank you card writing station. The full-time staff was offered the opportunity to write messages for their students staff to express their gratitude for the formers' hard work throughout the semester. The design was created with Adobe Illustrator. The backdrop of the design contains three elements: watercolor, knit texture, and a neutral, beige color. The ornaments I decorated the work "love" with were carefully design to create a regal appearance.

Final Thoughts

Although this was a very overwhelming task for me to lead, as it was the very first time I was responsible for managing the marketing and design of the entire banquet, it was definitely an educational and insightful experience that I will repeat in a heartbeat. It was definitely an opportunity for me to exercise my creativity and develop a fundamental foundation in event planning. All in all, I am humbly impressed by the success of the event and I could not ask for a more harmonious banquet. And, one last thing, the backdrop used for the photo-booth station was also designed by me.
