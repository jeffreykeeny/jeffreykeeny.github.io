PARKING BUDDY

Take a moment and evaluate the average day of an average first-world human. What are the everyday pet-peeves that we know exist, but go unnoticed. Distinguish the bug - the aglets breaking off shoelaces, the ends of your iPhone charger breaking, the glasses on hanging on your collar falling off when you bend over, etc. Then, find a solution to it by generating a high fidelity prototype, after conducting user research and user testing. This was the prompt given to us for the Human Centered Design DeCal at the University of California Berkeley. Enter the designer aka me. For this project, I decided to tackle the biggest first-world problem: finding parking in the city.

Problem

Solution

Parking is difficult to find in urban cities, such as Los Angeles or San Francisco. Though parking options are available, it is difficult to find vacancy without spending a substantial amount of time and money. In addition, there is no way of predicting if a particular street or parking structure has vacant spaces available, adding to the time wasted finding a parking space. Lastly, street parking, which is prominent in metropolitans, can be a nuisance especially with the inability to conveniently refill the parking meters.

First, let’s establish a hypothetical world, in which parking meters are implemented with identification. This would be implemented through a unique string or a quick response code (QR code). With the identification in place, this mobile app will allow its users to find parking efficiently and conveniently. Furthermore, it will also allow users to refill the parking meter remotely, eliminating the stress of worrying when the parking meter is going to strike.

User Experience

In this section, I have displayed the first and final iterations of the app side-by-side, with notes highlighting the main points collected from the user tests. I conducted the user tests on 20 individuals, all of whom were selected from the surveyed group. The user tests were performed on InVision.

Branding

The branding of the Parking Buddy was relatively straightforward, with the colors being the only tricky aspect. When designing the color palette of the app, it was important - in my opinion - to maintain a neutral scheme that appealed to all users alike. To my surprise, when asked what colors from the rainbow I should use for the app's design, the majority of the users suggested to use either vivid blue or stay with neutral colors, with an accent. Eventually, I came up with this colors scheme; I thought it remained true to the neutrality color palette, but still added some character with the pop of colors.

As for the typeface, I thought it was important to give it character, as stated before, and so I decided to use a slab serif - Museo Slab. It is whimsical and sophisticated.

 

The logo design was simple and self-explanatory. My design style is very minimal, and I figured this would make the perfect design. I decided to use a gradient fill on the letters in the background to give the design a bit more dimension.

Final Product

The final prototype was presented in front of the Human Centered Design DeCal (approximately 30 students) and two human centered designers. The product was not only captivating, but also a feasibly useful application.

 

The feedback I received were mostly positive, with more than 90% of the audience suggesting they would use Parking Buddy. "This very well designed, with aesthetics and functionality in mind. It's intuitive and easy for any first-time user to be well acquainted with."

 

However, it is not perfect and did receive some deserved constructive criticism - opening the doors for improvement. Most of the critiques revolved around the accountability of an user using Parking Buddy. How does PB notify users of an open spot with a running meter? How can PB prevent users from hogging a spot? These are some questions that were discussed during the showcase.

 

Nevertheless, I am proud of this app and I can absolutely see this being a plausible and effective concept in the near future. Although I have yet to enhance the functionality of the product since the showcase, I am satisfied with what I have created thus far.
