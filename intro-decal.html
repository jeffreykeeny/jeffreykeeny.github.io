The DeCal Program

 

The DeCal Program (or just DeCal) is an aggregate of student-run courses at the University of California, Berkeley. In support of democratic education, students create and facilitate their own classes on a variety of subjects, many of which are not addressed in the traditional curriculum.

 

Around 3000-4000 UC Berkeley students take DeCals each semester. DeCals are an excellent way of meeting the University’s minimum unit requirement, developing a new or past interest, and meeting peers in a small, comfortable environment. The DeCal Program is undoubtedly one of the many unique aspects of Berkeley’s undergraduate program.

 

Through Innovative Design, I received the amazing opportunity to facilitate and lead the Introduction to Photoshop & Illustrator DeCal within the College of Engineering at UC Berkeley.

What is my role?

As the instructor for this course, it is my responsibility to facilitate a twelve week-long class comprised of approximately thirty undergraduate students. The class may occasionally include graduate, study abroad, researchers, and UC Berkeley Extension Program students.

 

Comprised of four sections, the class is intended for students and aspiring designers who have little to no prior knowledge of graphic design and the Adobe softwares. The curriculum focuses on basic fundamental skills of visual and graphic design through educational lesson plans and hands-on tutorials with the use of Adobe Creative Cloud.

 

During the two hour-long class time, students will spend the first hour understanding the program and the accompanying tools. Each lesson is specifically created to focus on a theme or set of related functions that the programs offer, such as Masking, Blending Options, Typography, etc. Then, the class will spend the second hour strictly on application of the tools they just learned. Innovative Design's Education Team has developed unique and instructive tutorials that will provide students with an understanding of how the tools can be used in a real world application, such as movie posters, infographics, and album art.

To get students started on their assignments, I will provide them with examples that I've created using the tools they learned in class. We also use other mediums as forms of inspiration, including Dribbble, BeHance, and Designspiration.

 

Throughout the semester, I also provide students with technical and aesthetic feedback to their work. This allows them to improve their designs immediately and more effectively. Additionally, I along with the other facilitators come together to complete administrative work, form the design curriculum, generate design activities that the students can participate in, and engage with students personally to help them hone their skills better.

 

Lastly, we also spend some time educating students on the principles of graphic design, with brief lessons on color theory, composition, and branding.

Just for the students.

At the end of the semester, Innovative Design holds a special public showcase to present the students' works from throughout the course. Through this, we hope to allow students to feel accomplishment and gratitude for the hard work they put in all semester. We combine all the sections and  also invite the internal design community to come and admire the students' creations, while providing positive and constructive feedback. This allows for a final collaboration before the students then reflect on their own works and artistic development.
