This was a photograph taken of my hometown. The goal was to demonstrate a sci-fi style using photo manipulation. The original photograph was edited using Adobe Lightroom, and the moon was created using Adobe Photoshop. The surface of the moon was created from a photo of a boulder I had taken, and spherized in Photoshop. Light leaks and smoke effects were also incorporated into the final design to seamlessly integrate the moon behind the hills.

This project was created for UC Berkeley’s Theta Chi Fraternity Mu Chapter. I was asked to design a flyer for the fraternity’s Spring 2014 recruitment. The goal for the presentation of the card was to portray a sense of allure and mystery. The color scheme is very neutral, allowing for the military red, Theta Chi's color, text to stand out explicitly from the other details of the graphic. I worked extensively with the gaussian blur effect throughout the design, particularly to create the artificial bokeh effect. The silhouette of the audience at the bottom of the design was created from a photograph I took at a concert. I meticulously cropped the audience to isolate the silhouette. Then, I adjusted the exposure of the selection to create the silhouette effect.

 

This is a personal project I took on for me to practice my photo manipulation. The subject of the photograph is Vincent, whose blue eyes are manipulated to illustrate a galactic reflection. I utilized photographs provided by NASA and integrated into his iris to create a simple, but timeless effect. The lighting and color effects of the entire photo was edited using Adobe Lightroom.

This is a personal project I completed on my free time, and primarily demonstrates my photography manipulation expertise. I adjusted the lighting and color balance using Adobe Lightroom, and removed any unwanted objects from the original photo using Adobe Photoshop. The most conspicuous object digitally removed from the original photograph is the woman behind the subject. The tools that were used include the clone, brush, and patch tools.
